# Market-Making Game — Rules

---

## The Deck

A deck of **50 cards**:

- Two copies each of **0, 1, 2, … 20** (42 cards)
- One each of **−10, −20, −30, −40, −50, −60, −70, −80** (8 cards)

> **Hint:** The sum of all cards in the deck is **+60**.

---

## Setup

1. Shuffle the full deck.
2. Deal **one private card** to each team, except one team which receives **two cards**.
3. Do **not** reveal your cards to other teams.
4. Place **6 cards face-down** in the center of the table — these are the **table cards**.

---

## Objective

Each team's goal is to **make a market on the sum of the 6 table cards**. Use the information from:

- Your own private cards
- The signals others reveal through their trading decisions

to estimate the true sum as accurately as possible and trade profitably around it.

---

## Key Concepts

### The Market
Each market consists of two numbers posted simultaneously:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Bid** | The price at which you are willing to **buy** |
| **Ask** | The price at which you are willing to **sell** |

- All markets must be exactly **3 points wide** (ask = bid + 3).
- The market-maker must show size of **at least 1 contract** but may offer more.
- Any other team may **lift the ask** (buy from the market-maker) or **hit the bid** (sell to the market-maker).
- The team that **trades** is obligated to post the **next market**.
- The final sum — and therefore all market prices — **can be negative**.

### The Facilitator Rule
If no team is willing to trade, the facilitator steps in and makes a trade. The facilitator will make a **profitable trade 75% of the time**. The team that trades with the facilitator must then post a new market.

> Trading with the facilitator gives you information — at a cost.

---

## Scoring

All trades settle against the **final revealed sum** of the 6 table cards once the game ends.

**Profit = Trade Price − Final Sum** (for the seller)  
**Profit = Final Sum − Trade Price** (for the buyer)

**Example:**  
You sell at **15**. The final sum is **4**.  
→ You gain **11 points**. The buyer loses **11 points**.

Scores accumulate across all trades throughout the game. The team with the **highest total score wins**.

---

## Round Structure

### Round 1 — No table cards revealed

- Each team holds their private card(s).
- The team holding **two private cards** opens the game by posting the first market, **3 points wide**.
- Trading proceeds until no further trades are made, then the round ends.

### Round 2 — 2 table cards flipped; 1 new private card dealt to each team

- Flip **2 of the 6 table cards** face-up.
- Deal **1 additional private card** to each team.
- The team that **traded last in Round 1** opens Round 2 with a **3-point-wide market**.

### Round 3 — 2 more table cards flipped; 1 new private card dealt to each team

- Flip **2 more table cards** face-up (4 table cards now visible).
- Deal **1 additional private card** to each team.
- The team that **traded last in Round 2** opens Round 3 with a **3-point-wide market**.

### End of Game

- Flip the **final 2 table cards** face-up — all 6 table cards are now revealed.
- Calculate the **final sum** of the 6 table cards.
- Settle all trades. Calculate final scores.
- The team with the **highest cumulative score wins**.

| 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 pts | Last trader from Round 1 |
| 3 | 4 | 3 | 3 pts | Last trader from Round 2 |
| End | 6 | — | — | Settle all trades |
